# Phonology

Ethra has 5 vowels and 20 consonants. The inventory is small enough for broad pronunciation while still allowing contrast between soft, intimate sound and hard, commanding sound.

## Vowels

```text
a /a/  father
e /e/  clear e
i /i/  machine
o /o/  more
u /u/  rule
```

Vowels are stable. There is no English-style vowel drift in spelling.

## Consonants

```text
b d f g h j k l m n p r s t v w x y z c
```

Special values:

- `c` is always /sh/ as in "she".
- `j` is always /dzh/ as in "judge".
- `x` is /kh/ or a strong h. Casual speech may soften it to `h`, but formal pronunciation keeps it distinct.

## Syllables

Allowed syllable shapes:

```text
CV
CVC
V
VC
```

Hyphenated compounds are pronounced with a light boundary:

```text
mi-rah
dav-da
so-fer
```

Inside a hyphen segment, Ethra avoids consonant clusters. This keeps words pronounceable and makes root patterns audible.

## Stress

Stress the first syllable of the main lexical word:

```text
RAH
mi-RAH
DAV-da
ha-ZUR
```

Prefixes such as `mi-`, `ha-`, and `so-` are lighter than the root.

## Soft And Hard Sound

Soft sounds: `m n l r v w y h c`

Hard sounds: `p t k g d b s z j x`

Soft sound is favored in intimacy, grief, mercy, memory, and love. Hard sound is favored in law, building, command, correction, and public action.
