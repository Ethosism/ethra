# Examples

Canonical examples are stored in `spec/examples.yaml` with literal translation, natural translation, grammar notes, and cultural notes.

## Core Sentences

```text
1. Ke rah na.
   See me.

2. Ke sol-rah na.
   Really see me.

3. Leb en na e kot rih.
   My heart is an open book.

4. Na va pat ko ti ad ned wed.
   I would walk with you to the end of the world.

5. Ke mar yes ta.
   Remember who you are.

6. Na vel dev se.
   I choose this duty.

7. Na mor dev se.
   I inherited this duty.

8. Na dov tar mo mik.
   I vow to repair what was broken.

9. Ke ban mo fer ka dav.
   Build what the future has already claimed.

10. Lem ga naf bi mar.
    The people survive by remembering.

11. Mev e reh.
    Love is recognition.

12. Nem ka nam e dev mor.
    A spoken word becomes a carried burden.

13. Na val so-na.
    I act for myself.

14. Na val so-hen.
    I act for my family.

15. Na val so-lem.
    I act for my people.

16. Na val so-rah.
    I act before truth.

17. Tu lun nom en li, li fu dav ad fer.
    The child receives the name and becomes responsible to the future.

18. Sel ne ko mer e xed.
    Freedom without memory becomes emptiness.

19. Kev ne ko dev e mak-da.
    Power without duty becomes corruption.

20. Lem hid dak lem en li mo san.
    A superior culture teaches its people what to notice.
```

Use the CLI for full notes:

```bash
cargo run --quiet -- translate-example 8
```
