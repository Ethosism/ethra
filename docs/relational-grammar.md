# Relational Grammar

Ethra pronouns encode stance. They are not caste ranks. A speaker chooses the form that best clarifies the relationship and responsibility in the moment.

## Second Person Forms

```text
ti   you as intimate beloved
ta   you as friend or equal
to   you as elder or teacher
tu   you as child or student
te   you as citizen or civic peer
za   you as opponent
hu   you as sacred addressee
tum  you as collective people
```

Examples:

```text
Ti rah na.
You, beloved, see me.

Te pu nam.
You, civic peer, speak publicly.

Za sak so-rah.
You, opponent, be judged before truth.
```

## First And Third Person

```text
na   I, me
nel  we of chosen commitment
nen  we of household or kin
num  we as a people
li   third-person person, gender-neutral
lu   they
lo   it
hon  the household as a unit
```

Gender is not mandatory. It can be named lexically when emotionally, biologically, legally, or poetically meaningful.

## Principle

The goal is precision, not submission. `to` honors an elder or teacher because the relation carries responsibility. `tu` addresses a child or student with care, not contempt. `za` names conflict without stripping the opponent of personhood.
