# Moral Grammar

Ethra's main innovation is moral grammar. It makes agency distinctions short and ordinary.

## Agency Particles

```text
kan     I can, I have capacity
lun     I may, I am permitted
wen     I want, I desire
vel     I choose
cel     I should, it is fitting
dom     I owe
dov     I vow
ten     I am entrusted with
mor     I inherited this duty
tor     I chose this duty
ren     I repair what was broken
```

These are not stylistic synonyms:

```text
Na kan tar.  I can repair.
Na lun tar.  I may repair.
Na wen tar.  I want to repair.
Na vel tar.  I choose to repair.
Na cel tar.  I should repair.
Na dom tar.  I owe repair.
Na dov tar.  I vow to repair.
Na mor tar.  I inherited repair-duty.
```

## Scope Of Action

```text
so-na    for myself
so-hen   for family
so-lem   for people/civilization
so-fer   for the future
so-rah   before truth
so-zur   before the sacred or ultimate
```

Examples:

```text
Na val so-na.
I act for myself.

Na val so-lem.
I act for my people.

Na val so-rah.
I act before truth.
```

## Cultural Function

English often lets "I want," "I choose," "I should," and "I owe" blur into each other. Ethra separates them so the speaker must locate agency. This is meant to reduce shallow individualism and bureaucratic evasion: a speaker can still act selfishly, but the scope can be named.
