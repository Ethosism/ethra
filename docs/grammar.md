# Grammar

Ethra grammar is mostly analytic. Words do not carry heavy conjugation; particles carry tense, aspect, mood, and moral stance.

## Basic Order

Default order:

```text
Subject - particles - verb - object - scope/complements
```

Examples:

```text
Na vel dev se.
I choose this duty.

Lem ga naf bi mar.
The people survive by remembering.
```

## Tense And Aspect

```text
pa  past
nu  present/progressive
fu  future
ka  completed
ga  ongoing/habitual
va  intended/conditional
```

Particles precede the verb:

```text
Na pa mar.
I remembered.

Na fu tar.
I will repair.

Na va pat ko ti.
I would walk with you.
```

## Copula And Negation

```text
e   is, equals, be
ne  not
```

```text
Mav e reh.
Love is recognition.

Kev ne ko dev e mak-da.
Power without duty becomes corruption.
```

## Nouns

Plurality is optional unless important. Add `lu` after a noun when plurality must be explicit.

Possession uses `en`:

```text
Leb en na
heart of me, my heart
```

Definiteness is contextual. `se` means this.

## Questions And Commands

```text
ya  question
ke  imperative
```

Word order stays stable:

```text
Ya ta mar?
Do you remember?

Ke rah na.
See me.
```
